Second and Third Conditionals


Second conditional: We use the it to describe things that may be impossible or are just a fantasy.
Example: If I won the lottery I would buy a big house.
Formula: If + past simple, ...would + infinitive

Third Conditional: We use it to talk about the past and situations that didn't happen, and to imagine the result of this situation.
Example: If I had seen Mary, I would have told her.
Formula: if + past perfect, ...would + have + past participle.
